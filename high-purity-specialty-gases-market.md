<p><p>High purity specialty gases are classified into four main types: noble gases, carbon gases, halogen gases, and other specialty gases. Noble gases such as helium and argon are inert and stable, commonly used in welding and as insulators. Carbon gases like carbon monoxide and methane have various industrial applications, from chemical synthesis to semiconductor manufacturing. Halogen gases like chlorine and fluorine are used in disinfection and etching processes. Other specialty gases include rare gases, electronic gases, and cryogenic gases with specific applications in industries such as healthcare, electronics, and aerospace.</p></p>
