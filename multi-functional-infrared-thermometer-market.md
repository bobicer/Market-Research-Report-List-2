<p><p>The multi-functional infrared thermometer market includes both contact and non-contact infrared thermometers. Contact infrared thermometers require physical contact with the object being measured, while non-contact infrared thermometers can measure temperature from a distance. Contact infrared thermometers are commonly used for medical purposes, cooking, and industrial applications where precise measurements are needed. Non-contact infrared thermometers are ideal for situations where hygiene or safety concerns make physical contact impractical, such as monitoring temperatures of moving objects or in environments where contamination must be avoided.</p></p>
