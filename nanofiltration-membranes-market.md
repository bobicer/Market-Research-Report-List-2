<p><p>Nanofiltration membranes are categorized into three main types: polymeric, ceramic, and hybrid membranes. Polymeric membranes are made from synthetic polymers, offering high flexibility and low cost. Ceramic membranes are made from inorganic materials, providing excellent chemical and thermal stability. Hybrid membranes combine the characteristics of both polymeric and ceramic membranes, offering a balance between flexibility and durability. Each type of membrane has its own unique properties and advantages, making them suitable for various applications in water treatment and filtration processes.</p></p>
