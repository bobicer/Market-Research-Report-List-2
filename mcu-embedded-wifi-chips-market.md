<p><p>MCU-embedded WiFi chips come in two main market types: single band and dual band. Single band chips operate on a single frequency, allowing for basic WiFi connectivity. Dual band chips, on the other hand, can operate on two different frequencies simultaneously, providing faster speeds and improved performance, especially in areas with high WiFi traffic. Both types of chips are commonly used in embedded systems such as IoT devices, smart appliances, and industrial machines to enable wireless communication and connectivity.</p></p>
