<p><strong>The Podiatry Micromotor Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Brushed</li><li>Brushless</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Podiatry micromotors come in two main types: brushed and brushless. Brushed micromotors have carbon brushes that transfer electrical current to the rotor, providing power for the motor. On the other hand, brushless micromotors use electronic commutation to control the speed and direction of the rotor without the need for brushes. Brushless micromotors are typically more efficient, have a longer lifespan, and produce less heat compared to brushed micromotors. Both types are commonly used in podiatry for precision and control during procedures.</p></p>
