<p><p>Semiconductor wafer electrostatic chucks (ESCs) are used in semiconductor manufacturing to hold wafers in place during processing. There are two main types of ESCs: Coulomb type and Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) type. Coulomb type ESCs use electromechanical force to hold the wafer in place, while JR type ESCs use a combination of electrostatic and mechanical forces. Both types are crucial in ensuring precise positioning and stability of wafers during the manufacturing process, leading to higher yield and quality of semiconductor devices.</p></p>
