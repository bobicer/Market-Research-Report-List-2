<p><p>The automotive proton exchange membrane (PEM) fuel cell market includes three main types based on hydrogen storage methods: compressed gaseous hydrogen, cryogenic liquid hydrogen, and hydrides. Compressed gaseous hydrogen involves storing hydrogen at high pressure in tanks, while cryogenic liquid hydrogen involves storing it in liquid form in cryogenic tanks. Hydrides utilize solid materials to store hydrogen through a chemical reaction. Each type has its own advantages and limitations, impacting the overall operation and efficiency of PEM fuel cells in automotive applications.</p></p>
