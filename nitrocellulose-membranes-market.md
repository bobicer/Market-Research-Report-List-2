<p><p>Nitrocellulose membranes are categorized based on their pore sizes. Membranes with pore sizes below 0.45 µm are used for ultrafiltration applications, while those with pore sizes ranging from 0.45 to 6 µm are suitable for microfiltration. Membranes with pore sizes above 6 µm are designed for macrofiltration purposes. Each type of membrane caters to specific filtration needs in various industries such as pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, and food and beverage processing. The choice of membrane pore size depends on the desired level of filtration and separation efficiency.</p></p>
