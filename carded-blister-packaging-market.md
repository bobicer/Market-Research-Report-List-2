<p><p>Carded blister packaging is a type of packaging that features a thermoformed or cold-formed plastic blister attached to a backing card. Thermoforming involves heating a plastic sheet and forming it over a mold to create the blister, while cold forming uses pressure to form the blister without heat. Thermoforming is commonly used for standard packaging applications due to its cost-effectiveness, while cold forming is preferred for products that require extra protection or a more premium look.</p></p>
